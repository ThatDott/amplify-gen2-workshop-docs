    Cognito User Management¶

    Estimated time to read: 2 minutes

    Once you’ve added authentication to your application using Amplify, your backend is essentially powered by Amazon Cognito under-the-hood. This securely manages user accounts, authentication flows, and user data. In this section, we’ll explore how to view and manage users directly in the Amplify Console.

    What is Amazon Cognito?¶

    Amazon Cognito is a fully managed service that handles:

    • User sign-up and sign-in
    • Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
    • User pools (authentication) and identity pools (authorization)
    • Social logins (Google, Facebook, Apple, etc.)
    • Secure token generation for your frontend apps

    When you define an Auth resource in Amplify, Cognito automatically creates the required User Pool and Identity Pool in the cloud sandbox.

    Viewing Users in the Amplify Console¶

    After deploying your auth resource via npx ampx sandbox, you can view and manage users from the Amplify Dashboard.

    1. Open the Amplify Console in your browser.
    2. Select your deployed app from the list of applications.
    3. Choose the deployed branch you want to manage (e.g., main or dev).
    4. Click on “Authentication” in the sidebar to access your Auth resources and view all users.

    Managing Users¶

    In the Authentication section of the Amplify Console, you can view all registered users. By clicking on a specific user, you can access detailed information and manage their account directly.

    • View usernames, email addresses, and sign-up status
    • Enable or disable accounts
    • Reset user passwords
    • Force email verification
    • Assign users to groups for role-based access control

    This interface gives you full visibility and control over the users in your app without writing any backend code.
